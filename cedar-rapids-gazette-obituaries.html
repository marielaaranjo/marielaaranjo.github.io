Reading and Understanding Obituaries

Obituaries can provide a wealth of information about the deceased and their life. However, understanding and interpreting the details presented in an obituary can sometimes be challenging. The Structure of an Obituary

Obituaries typically follow a specific structure that includes essential details about the deceased. Understanding this structure will help you navigate through the information provided and extract the most relevant details.

Here is a breakdown of the typical structure of an obituary:

Name and Basic Information

The obituary begins with the full name of the deceased, including any nicknames or preferred names they might have used. It also includes their age at the time of death and the date and place of their passing.

Biographical Information

This section provides a summary of the deceased's life, including their date and place of birth, parents' names, and any significant milestones or achievements. It may also mention their education, career, and hobbies.

Family Members

The obituary typically lists immediate family members, including spouses, children, and grandchildren. It may also include information about siblings, parents, and other close relatives. The names of predeceased family members may be mentioned as well.

Funeral Arrangements

This section provides details about the funeral or memorial service, including the date, time, and location. It may also mention any specific requests from the family regarding donations or flowers.

Final Thoughts and Tributes

In this section, friends and family members often express their thoughts and share memories of the deceased. It's a space for loved ones to pay tribute, offer condolences, and share their grief with the community.

Deciphering Abbreviations and Symbols

Obituaries may contain abbreviations and symbols that can be confusing if you're not familiar with their meanings. Here are some commonly used abbreviations and symbols found in obituaries:

RIP

Rest in peace. This symbolizes a wish for the deceased to find eternal peace after death.

DOB

Date of birth. This indicates the date on which the deceased was born.

DOD

Date of death. This signifies the date on which the deceased passed away.

POB

Place of birth. This refers to the location where the deceased was born.

POD

Place of death. This indicates the location where the deceased passed away.

Survived By

This phrase is often used to introduce the section that lists immediate family members who are still alive.

Predeceased By

This phrase is used to introduce the section that lists family members who passed away before the deceased.

Extracting Meaningful Information

Pay attention to the biographical information, as it provides insights into the person's life and achievements. Take note of any significant milestones, hobbies, or passions they had. This information can help you connect with the deceased on a personal level and appreciate their unique qualities.

Highlight Key Life Events

Next, highlight key life events and achievements of the deceased. This can include their date and place of birth, educational background, professional accomplishments, and any significant contributions they made to their community or field of work.

For instance:

Bob was born on September 10, 1949, in Cedar Rapids, Iowa. Bob had a successful career as a financial advisor, helping countless individuals and businesses achieve their financial goals.

Celebrate Personal Passions and Hobbies

Include information about the deceased's personal passions, hobbies, and interests. This provides a glimpse into their personality and allows readers to connect with them on a more personal level.

For example:

Outside of work, Bob had a deep love for the outdoors. He spent his weekends hiking in the nearby Cedar Rapids nature trails, fishing in the local rivers, and tending to his beautiful garden. Bob's green thumb was admired by all, and he often shared his gardening tips and plants with friends and neighbors.

Acknowledge Family and Loved Ones

In this section, acknowledge and honor the immediate family members of the deceased. Include the names of their spouse, children, grandchildren, and any other close relatives. This allows readers to understand the family dynamics and express their condolences appropriately.

For instance:

Bob is survived by his loving wife of 50 years, Susan Johnson, their two children, David and Emily, and his three grandchildren, Sarah, Michael, and Olivia. He is also survived by his sister, Mary Thompson, and his brother, James Johnson.

Provide Funeral or Memorial Service Details

Include the details of the funeral or memorial service, such as the date, time, and location. If the family has any specific requests, such as donations to a charity or flowers, mention them in this section as well.

For example:

A celebration of Bob's life will be held on July 20, 2022, at 10:00 a.m. at the First United Methodist Church in Cedar Rapids. In lieu of flowers, the family kindly requests donations to be made to the Cedar Rapids Nature Center, a cause that was dear to Bob's heart.

Encourage Remembrances and Tributes

In this final section, encourage friends, family, and community members to share their remembrances and tributes to the deceased. This provides an opportunity for others to express their condolences and share their memories, further honoring the life of the departed.

For instance:

The family invites friends and loved ones to share their memories and tributes to Bob on the online guestbook at www.cedarrapidsgazette.com/obituaries. Your kind words and stories will provide comfort during this difficult time.

Writing an obituary is a deeply personal and emotional task, and it's essential to approach it with sensitivity and respect. Take the time to reflect on the life and legacy of the deceased, and craft a heartfelt tribute that honors their memory.
