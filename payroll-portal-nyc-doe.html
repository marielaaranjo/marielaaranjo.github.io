The earnings section of your pay stub provides a breakdown of your gross pay for the pay period. It includes details such as your hourly rate, number of hours worked, and any additional compensation or overtime pay. This section helps you understand how your earnings are calculated.

Taxes

In the taxes section, you will find information about the various taxes withheld from your paycheck. This includes federal income tax, state income tax, and any local taxes applicable to your location. The pay stub also shows the total amount withheld for each tax and provides details about your filing status and exemptions.

Deductions

Deductions on your pay stub represent the amounts withheld from your gross pay for various purposes. Net Pay

The net pay section displays the final amount you will receive after all deductions and taxes have been withheld from your gross pay. This is the amount that will be deposited into your bank account through direct deposit or issued to you as a physical paycheck.

Year-to-Date (YTD) Summary

The YTD summary on your pay stub provides a cumulative overview of your earnings, taxes, and deductions from the beginning of the calendar year. It helps you track your income, tax payments, and contributions over time.

Understanding the components of your pay stub on the payroll portal NYC DOE ensures that you have a clear picture of your earnings and deductions, enabling you to manage your finances effectively.

The payroll portal NYC DOE withholds Social Security and Medicare taxes, commonly referred to as FICA taxes.
